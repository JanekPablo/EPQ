Timeline

Diary of the my EPQ process, outline the initial planning and then the development of an idea taking into account the main hurdles I had to overcome and major changes to my plan.  

9 - 15  november 2017

Planning

Mind Map: ideas along the lines of passions, Woodwork, Physics, Engineering and Photography. My current Favourite is a Photography Project with working title, Produce and develop a series of film photos. As an artefact EPQ this would allow me to focus on the chemical darkroom process and traditional dodge and burn methods of photography which interests me. The project needs to be discussed with my photography teacher, to determine whether the topic could result in dual accreditation. In the meantime I will do some further research into this idea to decide myself if I think it is feasible and to understand the amount of knowledge required to complete the project. Emailed photography teacher to arrange a meeting.

14-15 november 2017

Initial Research

Researched around the current working title Produce and develop a series of film photos. What Style of film photography do I want to focus on? What Chemicals would I need? What is the best Film to use? How do I develop and edit a picture in the darkroom does the school have the required equipment? etc. From my primary research it seems there is a large source of web based resources on how to develop film but the process isn’t easy and would require a large amount of time to practice and perfect which could be difficult considering the darkroom is on school site and is not always free for student use. The project provides enough content to research and to explore various methods it would also allow for development and testing stages.

18 november 2017

Meeting 

Discussed the EPQ with Photography teacher Mr Feijoo. He thinks the project would be fine in that the in depth process of film development specifically wouldn’t be covered in our course. The School also has all the equipment necessary to complete the project on site. He has also offered to help with understanding the process so I could talk to him as part of the research.

20 november 2017

Change of Plan

I am now taking the full A-Level photography course, this means the project although still not clashing in terms of the specification, wouldn’t allow for as much in terms of the scope of development as I will have covered a lot of the film photographers who interest me in the A-Level photography course. Need to think of another project idea.

24 november 2017

Planning

24/10/17
The Mind map I produced on the 10/10/17 had a variety of ideas however didn’t include one I felt very passionately about. Having discussed the EPQ project with a couple of physics teachers to get an idea of what I could do, one of my teachers suggested I focused a project on learning a coding language. So with this in mind I decided I would start my project on the idea of developing a light meter, which takes lux value and converts it to shutter and aperture. This would combine a lot of my interests. The problem solving aspect of writing code appealed to me and learning this new skill could be very useful, when going to Uni. As within lots of STEM subject at university they start teaching some basic coding languages, so a prior knowledge could come in handy. Furthermore this would combine knowledge of the workings of a camera, however more in depth than is covered in our photography lessons.

28 november 2017

Research 

Today I did some broad research on what coding language I may learn and the maths required to make a working light meter just to determine weather the project is feasible. From my research it doesn’t seem to look too difficult however I plan to discuss my idea with my EPQ supervisor.
